with quantum computing on the horizon rsa and elliptic curve cryptography ecc which
secure cyberspace for financial institutions are under serious threat many important financial
groups are getting ready for the new cryptographic age but us community banks and smes
are not as prepared as they should be even with few technical tools such institutions have an

community banks and financial smes it evaluates cryptographic risks checks the capability

details published on cases the framework mainly uses strategies incorporated in nists pqc

trustworthy as quantum computing gains importance

 risk prioritization analysis
by applying the risk matrix integrating likelihood and impact institutions were categorized
into three risk zones reflecting their urgency for pqc transition
 highrisk group 
institutions in this group face critical vulnerabilities due to heavy reliance on legacy
cryptographic protocols without any mitigation strategies they predominantly
operate aging core systems possess weak cryptographic inventories and have limited
awareness or budgetary support these banks are at elevated risk for potential
breaches once quantum adversaries emerge threatening operational continuity and
customer data security
 mediumrisk group 
constituting the largest segment these institutions acknowledge the quantum threat
but lack comprehensive migration plans or technical pilot projects their risk is
mitigated somewhat by governance awareness or incremental investments in
cybersecurity yet they remain vulnerable in the medium term if pqc migration is not
accelerated
 lowrisk group 
these institutions are pioneers among community banks and smes actively
experimenting with hybrid cryptographic systems or implementing thorough
cryptographic inventories aligned with nist guidelines their proactive approach
reduces immediate risk but requires continued vigilance and investment to maintain
resilience
this risk stratification highlights the uneven state of pqc readiness and the critical need for
prioritized support to highrisk institutions

systems are secured around the world the study looks at how prepared us community banks
and financial smes are since these institutions form essential parts of the economy in both
rural and underserved areas our results point out that although awareness of quantum threats
is rising very few pqc measures have been applied in practical use
because rsa and ecc systems are often used and these are both at risk from quantum attacks
financial smes are left very open to attack because there arent broad inventories of
cryptographic methods along with unawareness among organizations incomplete budgets and
not enough technical support there arent many advances in moving to quantumresistant
systems because institutions are not yet prepared both they and the financial sector as a whole
face a new kind of risk as quantum computing advances
